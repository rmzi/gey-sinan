# Community Contributions Guide

## Why Your Help Matters

Harari is an endangered language with approximately 25,000 speakers worldwide. Every contribution — a recorded word, a verified spelling, a shared proverb — helps preserve the language for future generations.

Gey Sinan is built by and for the Harari community. Here's how you can help.

## Ways to Contribute

### 1. Record Your Voice

**At community events**: Visit the recording station. It takes 5-10 minutes to record 50 words. Your voice becomes the pronunciation guide that learners hear.

**Remotely**: Open the app's recording station on your phone or computer. Register as a speaker, consent to how your recordings will be used, and start recording words at your own pace.

What makes a good recording:
- Quiet environment (no TV, music, or crowd noise)
- Clear, natural pronunciation — speak as you normally would
- One word at a time, with a brief pause before and after

### 2. Verify Words

The app's vocabulary comes from academic dictionaries and community sources, but many entries need verification by native speakers.

Help us by checking:
- **Spellings**: Is the Latin transliteration accurate? Does the Ge'ez script look right?
- **Meanings**: Is the English translation correct? Are there better or more common translations?
- **Usage**: Is this word still commonly used? Is it formal, informal, or archaic?
- **Variants**: Do you know a different pronunciation or spelling used in your community?

### 3. Suggest Content

The app needs more than single words. Share:
- **Phrases**: Common expressions used in daily Harari conversation
- **Proverbs**: Traditional sayings and their meanings
- **Cultural vocabulary**: Words for traditional foods, practices, celebrations, and crafts
- **Conversational patterns**: How do you greet someone? Ask for directions? Order at the market?
- **Children's vocabulary**: Words and phrases that parents and grandparents teach children

### 4. Test the App

Use the app and tell us what works and what doesn't:
- Are the lessons too easy or too hard?
- Do the exercises feel natural?
- Is anything confusing about the interface?
- Does the app work well on your phone?
- Are there words you expected to find but didn't?

### 5. Share with Others

The app is most valuable when the community knows about it:
- Share with family members, especially younger generations
- Mention it at community events and gatherings
- Post about it in Harari community groups online
- Encourage elders to participate in recording sessions

## Future Roles

As the project grows, we'll need community members in these roles:

### Content Moderator
Review and approve community-submitted words, phrases, and corrections. Ensure accuracy and cultural appropriateness.

### Dialect Documentarian
Harari has dialectal variation. Help us document and tag regional differences in pronunciation and vocabulary.

### Curriculum Advisor
Help design lessons that reflect real community needs. What do diaspora youth most need to learn? What should come first?

### Event Organizer
Host recording sessions at local Harari community gatherings in your city.

## Contact

To contribute, verify words, or get involved, reach out through the app or community channels.

Every word preserved is a bridge between generations.
