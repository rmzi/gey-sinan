# Harari Language: Academic Research & Scholarly Resources

## Key Scholars

### Wolf Leslau (1906-2006)
The most prolific scholar of Harari linguistics. Major works include:
- **Etymological Dictionary of Harari** - Contains ~2,200 roots with etymologies and comparisons to other Ethiopic and Semitic languages
  - [PDF Download](https://everythingharar.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/LesluHarariDictionary145.pdf)
- *Harari* (1970)
- *The Harari Expression of While* (1994)

### Ewald Wagner
- *The Negative Imperfect in Ancient and Modern Harari* (1997)
- *Harari-Texte in Arabischer Schrift* (1983) - Wiesbaden: Franz Steiner

### Enrico Cerulli
- *Studi etiopici I. La lingua e la storia di Harar* (1936) - Rome: Istituto per l'Oriente

### Gideon Goldenberg
- *Nominalization and Adjectivization in Amharic and Harari* (1983)

## Key Research Findings

1. **Classification Debate**: Bulakh and Kogan reject the "Transversal Southern" grouping that would link Amharic-Argobba with Harari-East Gurage

2. **Cushitic Influence**: Wolf Leslau identified unique Cushitic loanwords found only in Harari, suggesting possible Cushitic roots

3. **Two Variants**: Harari has two variants - ancient (Old Harari/Classical) and modern

4. **Somali Connection**: Old Harari, as a literary language of Harar, has close linguistic ties to Somali due to the historical context of Harar as an Islamic hub

## Research Gaps

- Comprehensive documentation of dialects/registers
